Why Begin with Viśvam

विश्वम् · विष्णुः · भूतभव्यभवत्प्रभुः

viśvam · viṣṇuḥ · bhūtabhavyabhavatprabhuḥ

Yudhiṣṭhira is broken. The war is won, the kingdom is his, and the cost is unbearable. He asks Bhīṣma, dying on the bed of arrows, six desperate questions — who is the one deity in the world? what is the one refuge? by chanting what is a creature freed from the bondage of birth and death?

Bhīṣma does not answer with sermon. He does not argue. He does not console. He answers with names. A thousand of them.

And the very first one is viśvam — the universe. Vast. Impersonal. Cold. Not warmth. Not refuge. Not yet.

Why?

⊕

Viśvam — the universe. All that has been entered.

विशते प्रविश्यते वा सः विश्वः

Śaṅkara: viśate praviśyate vā, sa viśvaḥ — "that which pervades all, or into which all enter — hence viśva." The first word is not Viṣṇu's name as a person; it is the field of his presence. Before the deity is announced, the *occupied totality* is named.

The Ṛgveda 1.156.3 stands behind: cognizant of his greatness, celebrate his name; may we, O Viṣṇu, enjoy thy favour. The Vedic Viṣṇu is the strider who measured the worlds with three steps — the vyāpti of presence, not the warmth of a face.

◉

Viṣṇuḥ — the all-pervader. The one who.

विष्णुर्विष्णोतीति विष्णुः, सर्वव्याप्तेः

Śaṅkara: viṣṇur viṣṇotīti viṣṇuḥ, sarvavyāpteḥ — "Viṣṇu is so-named from √viṣ, 'to pervade.' He is unlimited by space, time, and substance."

Having named the universe, the stotra now names the universe's substrate. Viśvam answered what; viṣṇuḥ answers who. Two words in succession, and the deity is identified with the totality itself — not as someone within it, but as the pervading principle by which the totality exists at all.

This is vibhutva declared. The lakṣaṇa of universal pervasion, named in the second syllable.

∞

Bhūtabhavyabhavatprabhuḥ — Lord of what was, what will be, what is.

त्रिकालानां पतिः

Śaṅkara: trikālānāṃ patiḥ — lord of the three time-zones. The deity does not enter time; he stands over it. Prabhu — not kāla. He is not "in" the past or "in" the future. He governs both as their lord.

Space has been named (viśvam). Presence has been named (viṣṇuḥ). Now time — and time too is set under him, not around him. Three names. Three axes of one Brahman. The cosmological declaration is complete.

The Verse · BORI 13.135.14

विश्वं विष्णुर्वषट्कारो भूतभव्यभवत्प्रभुः ।
भूतकृद्भूतभृद्भावो भूतात्मा भूतभावनः ॥

viśvaṃ viṣṇur vaṣaṭkāro bhūtabhavyabhavatprabhuḥ / bhūtakṛd bhūtabhṛd bhāvo bhūtātmā bhūtabhāvanaḥ

The first śloka of the Sahasranāma. Nine names. Not one asks for anything. Not one offers warmth. The deity is announced as what exceeds the human being entirely.

Where Viśvam Sits — The Three Super-Movements

The Sahasranāma moves in three immense sweeps. Hear the difference.

I · Opens
viśvaṃ viṣṇur vaṣaṭkāro …
The universe, Viṣṇu, the sacrificial cry, the Lord of past, future, and present. Vast. Impersonal. Cosmic.
II · Middle
kāmahā kāmakṛt kāntaḥ kāmaḥ kāmapradaḥ prabhuḥ
Destroyer of desire, maker of desire, the beautiful one, desire itself, the granter of desire, the Lord. Intimate. Contradictory. Personal.
III · Closes
bhūr bhuvaḥ svas tarus tāraḥ …
Earth, atmosphere, heaven; the cosmic tree, the crosser, the sun. Five yajña-names in a ritual crescendo. Liturgical. Embodied. Performed.

The architecture is the teaching. The opening widens. The middle warms. The closing conducts the chant into worship.

The Why

Bhīṣma chose to begin cold. He did not begin with Kṛṣṇa, with Govinda, with Mādhava. He did not begin with the names a broken man wants to hear. He began with the universe.

"If one begins too quickly with intimacy, bhakti becomes sentiment. If one never leaves cosmology, devotion remains cold. The Sahasranāma first widens the heart, then warms it, then conducts it into worship."

This is the first move of a sahasranāma that knows what it is doing. Yudhiṣṭhira does not need consolation — he has tried that, and it failed. He needs to be repositioned. The universe must be re-seen as the body of the deity before any personal address means anything. Viśvam opens the eye. Viṣṇuḥ answers the eye. Bhūtabhavyabhavatprabhuḥ places past and future under one lordship — including the past Yudhiṣṭhira cannot bear and the future he must rule.

Only after this — only after the heart is widened to cosmic scale — does the stotra dare to speak of kāma, of Śrī, of Kṛṣṇa. The order is the medicine. The cold first, then the warmth, then the worship. Ekam sad viprā bahudhā vadanti: the one truth, spoken many ways — but each way placed exactly where it must be placed.

The Bhakti

To recite this triad with attention is to give up the demand that the divine be somewhere and sometime. Wherever the bhakta is, there is viśvam; the deity already pervades it. Whenever the bhakta is, the deity is prabhu — already lord of that time.

Reciting viśvam · viṣṇuḥ · bhūtabhavyabhavatprabhuḥ in succession is the cosmological initiation of the recitation. The chant has not yet narrowed into the personal; it is widening the bhakta into the universal. The medicine is the order. The order is the teaching. Begin with the universe, and the rest of the thousand names becomes possible.

From here the stotra walks, name by name, from viśvam at the first verse to sarvapraharaṇāyudhaḥ — armed with every weapon — at the last. From the universe to the armed form. From the abstract to the embodied. From the cold opening to the warm seeing.

Vedic Source · Nārāyaṇa Upaniṣad 1.13

अन्तर्बहिश्च तत्सर्वं व्याप्य नारायणः स्थितः

antar bahiś ca tat sarvaṃ vyāpya nārāyaṇaḥ sthitaḥ — Nārāyaṇa pervades all this, internally and externally.

The Upaniṣad's claim is the same claim the Sahasranāma's opening triad makes liturgical. Śaṅkara explicitly cites it in his gloss on viṣṇuḥ, with Ṛgveda 1.156.3 behind. The śruti underwrites the smṛti; the thousand names are not invention, they are recapitulation of what the Vedic strider has been from the beginning.
