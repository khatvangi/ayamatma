Viśvam · Viṣṇuḥ ·
Bhūtabhavyabhavatprabhuḥ

विश्वम् · विष्णुः · भूतभव्यभवत्प्रभुः

⊕
Viśvam — the universe; all that has been entered. विशते प्रविश्यते वा सः विश्वः Śaṅkara: viśate praviśyate vā, sa viśvaḥ — that which pervades all, or into which all enter — hence viśva.
◉
Viṣṇuḥ — the all-pervader. विष्णुर्विष्णोतीति विष्णुः, सर्वव्याप्तेः Śaṅkara: viṣṇur viṣṇotīti viṣṇuḥ, sarvavyāpteḥ — from √viṣ ('to pervade'). He is unlimited by space, time, and substance.
∞
Bhūtabhavyabhavatprabhuḥ — Lord of past, future, and present. त्रिकालानां पतिः Śaṅkara: trikālānāṃ patiḥ — lord of the three time-zones. He stands over time, not within it.
The Verse विश्वं विष्णुर्वषट्कारो भूतभव्यभवत्प्रभुः । भूतकृद्भूतभृद्भावो भूतात्मा भूतभावनः ॥ BORI Mbh 13.135.14 · VSN śloka 1
Vedic Source Nārāyaṇa Upaniṣad 1.13: अन्तर्बहिश्च तत्सर्वं व्याप्य नारायणः स्थितः — Nārāyaṇa pervades all this, internally and externally. Cited too at Ṛgveda 1.156.3, on which Śaṅkara grounds his gloss.
The Synthesis The opening triad names the deity along three axes at once. Viśvam names space-as-occupied — the totality that has been entered. Viṣṇuḥ names the occupier — the one who pervades. Bhūtabhavyabhavatprabhuḥ names time — as ruled, not entered into — he stands over the three time-zones as their lord, not as a creature inside them. Space-pervasion, active-pervader, time-supremacy: this is vibhutva — universal pervasion — declared in the very first three names, before the bhakta even reaches the second pāda of the first verse.
The Bhakti To recite this triad with attention is to give up the demand that the divine be somewhere and sometime. Wherever the bhakta is, there is viśvam; the deity already pervades it. Whenever the bhakta is, the deity is the prabhu — already lord of that time. Reciting names 1, 2, and 4 in succession is the cosmological initiation of the recitation: it places the bhakta into a universe whose substrate is divine, into a time whose lord is the deity.
