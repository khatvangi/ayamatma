## The Problem Nobody Asks

<Term iast="dhṛtarāṣṭra">Dhritarāṣṭra</Term> is physically blind. And yet the <Term iast="udyoga parva">Udyoga Parva</Term> records a moment where he asks <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya</Term> a question that cuts to the center of Advaita epistemology: *How do you recognize Krishna as <Term iast="paramātman">Paramātman</Term> when I cannot?*

This is an epistemological question, not a devotional one. <Term iast="dhṛtarāṣṭra">Dhritarāṣṭra</Term> is asking about the **instrument of recognition**, the cognitive and spiritual equipment that makes Brahman-recognition possible in one person and unavailable to another. <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya's</Term> answer, preserved in Udyoga Parva Section 69, is among the most epistemologically precise statements in the <Term iast="itihāsa">itihāsa</Term> tradition.

The answer's significance lies in what it excludes. Divine vision granted by grace, <Term iast="bhakti">bhakti</Term> as sufficient cause, witnessed miracles, accumulated merit: <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya</Term> cites none of these. His recognition of Krishna as <Term iast="brahman">Brahman</Term> rests on a single foundation: **<Term iast="ātma vidyā">Ātma Vidyā</Term>**, direct Upanishadic knowledge of the self.

<span class="pullquote">Miracles were present; devotion was present; merit had accumulated. None of these, alone or combined, produce recognition.</span>

This essay argues that <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya's</Term> epistemological stance — <Term iast="jñāna">jñāna</Term> as the primary <Term iast="pramāṇa">pramāṇa</Term> for Brahman-recognition — represents the position of the <Term iast="bhagavad gītā">Bhagavad Gītā</Term> itself, confirmed by the structural architecture of the Upanishads. It stands in direct contrast to the contemporary collapse of <Term iast="vedānta">Vedānta</Term> into devotional personalism and personality-cult.

## Sañjaya's Answer: Three Conditions, One Structure

In Section 69, <Term iast="dhṛtarāṣṭra">Dhritarāṣṭra</Term> presses <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya</Term> further: how does he maintain unbroken clarity about Krishna's identity across the turbulence of war-preparation, negotiation, and partisan pressure? <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya's</Term> response articulates three conditions. They form a single integrated architecture rather than a sequence of independent practices.

### First: non-orientation toward Māyā

<Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya</Term> does not say he overcame <Term iast="māyā">Māyā</Term>. He says he does not *worship* it — meaning he does not orient his cognitive attention toward <Term iast="sṛṣṭi">Sṛṣṭi</Term>, the created and shifting world, as the primary locus of reality. The Advaitic distinction between *<Term iast="vyāvahārika">vyāvahārika</Term>* (empirical standpoint) and *<Term iast="pāramārthika">pāramārthika</Term>* (absolute standpoint) functions here as a practised orientation applied continuously, rather than as a metaphysical classification deployed occasionally. <Term iast="māyā">Māyā</Term> carries its full Vedantic weight: the superimposition of multiplicity and becoming onto what is one and unchanging. The <Term iast="bhagavad gītā">Bhagavad Gītā</Term> states this precisely:

> <span class="verse-ref" data-verse="bg-7-14">*daivī hy eṣā guṇamayī mama māyā duratyayā / mām eva ye prapadyante māyām etāṃ taranti te*</span>

"This divine <Term iast="māyā">Māyā</Term> of mine, made of the <Term iast="guṇa">guṇas</Term>, is hard to cross. Those who take refuge in me alone cross beyond this <Term iast="māyā">Māyā</Term>." The operative phrase is *mām eva*, "in me alone," pointing toward the substratum rather than the products of <Term iast="māyā">Māyā</Term> or the contents of <Term iast="sṛṣṭi">Sṛṣṭi</Term>. The <Term iast="kaṭha upaniṣad">Kaṭha Upaniṣad</Term> frames the same choice as the Vedantic axis itself:

> <span class="verse-ref" data-verse="katha-1-2-2">*śreyas ca preyas ca manuṣyam etas / tau samparītya vivinakti dhīraḥ*</span>

"The good (*<Term iast="śreyas">śreyas</Term>*) and the pleasant (*<Term iast="preyas">preyas</Term>*) approach a person; the wise man, having examined both, distinguishes them." *<Term iast="preyas">Preyas</Term>* is <Term iast="māyā">Māyā's</Term> pull. *<Term iast="śreyas">Śreyas</Term>* is <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya's</Term> direction. The cosmological formulation of BG 7.14 and the psychological one of Kaṭha 1.2.2 describe the same choice.

### Second: Dharma anchored in Brahman-realization

<Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya</Term> refuses to practice <Term iast="dharma">Dharma</Term> that does not serve Brahman-realization — what the discourse identifies as *<Term iast="bhagavat arpaṇa">Bhagavat Arpaṇa</Term>*, dedication to the Divine as ultimate ground. <Term iast="dharma">Dharma</Term> performed for social function, familial obligation, or karmic accumulation he identifies as *<Term iast="vyartha">vyartha</Term>*, empty, without purchase on liberation.

The <Term iast="gītā">Gītā's</Term> resolution runs through its doctrine of *<Term iast="naiṣkarmya">naiṣkarmya</Term>*, action without the binding of result. Underneath <Term iast="naiṣkarmya">naiṣkarmya</Term> lies something more fundamental: every act in a realized life is already oriented toward the Real as ontological fact. The <Term iast="muṇḍaka upaniṣad">Muṇḍaka Upaniṣad</Term> states the structural limit of action-without-telos:

> <span class="verse-ref" data-verse="mundaka-1-2-12">*parīkṣya lokān karmacitān brāhmaṇo / nirvedamāyānnāstyakṛtaḥ kṛtena*</span>

"Having examined worlds won by action, a brahmin should arrive at non-attachment: what is not made cannot be reached by what is made." Action-as-such (*kṛta*) cannot reach the unconditioned (*akṛta*). <Term iast="karma-mārga">Karma-mārga</Term> requires <Term iast="jñāna-mārga">jñāna-mārga</Term> as its anchor, or it recycles indefinitely.

### Third: the unified sādhana

<Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya</Term> describes a *simultaneous configuration* — a mode of cognitive existence in which all four operate as a single instrument.

<span class="pullquote">Separated from jñāna, bhakti degenerates into personality-cult. Separated from bhakti, jñāna becomes inert.</span>

*<Term iast="śuddha manas">Śuddha Manas</Term>* (purified mind): the absence of the distortions that make Brahman-recognition impossible — specifically craving, aversion, and the misidentification of <Term iast="ātman">ātman</Term> with body-mind. Krishna's description at BG 10.11 is relevant: *<Term iast="jñāna-dīpena bhāsvatā">jñāna-dīpena bhāsvatā</Term>*, "with the shining lamp of knowledge." The metaphor presupposes a purified cognitive medium in which the lamp can shine; <Term iast="śuddha manas">Śuddha Manas</Term> is that medium.

*<Term iast="śraddhā">Śraddhā</Term>* (conviction): the <Term iast="vivekacūḍāmaṇi">Vivekacūḍāmaṇi</Term> (25) defines it, following the <Term iast="śaṅkara">Śaṅkara</Term> Bhāṣya tradition, as *śāstrasya guru-vākyasya satya-buddhy-avadhāraṇam*, the firm conviction that the words of scripture and teacher are true. The <Term iast="bhagavad gītā">Bhagavad Gītā</Term> confirms: <span class="verse-ref" data-verse="bg-4-39">*śraddhāvāl labhate jñānam tatparaḥ saṃyatendriyaḥ*</span> (BG 4.39), "the one of firm conviction, intent upon it, sense-controlled, obtains knowledge." The sequence is exact: <Term iast="śraddhā">śraddhā</Term> enables <Term iast="jñāna">jñāna</Term>. <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya's</Term> <Term iast="śraddhā">śraddhā</Term> is the epistemological posture that holds <Term iast="śabda-pramāṇa">śabda-pramāṇa</Term> as authoritative — which is why, when he looks at Krishna, he sees <Term iast="tattva">Tattva</Term> rather than a cousin or a diplomat.

*<Term iast="bhakti">Bhakti</Term>* (devotion): in <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya's</Term> architecture, <Term iast="bhakti">bhakti</Term> is cognitive adhesion to <Term iast="brahman">Brahman</Term> — the unbroken attention that the <Term iast="bṛhadāraṇyaka upaniṣad">Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad</Term> formulates as <span class="verse-ref" data-verse="brh-1-4-15">*ātmānam eva lokam upāsīta*</span> (Bṛ. 1.4.15), "let one worship the self alone as one's world." Together <Term iast="bhakti">bhakti</Term> and <Term iast="jñāna">jñāna</Term> constitute what BG 18.55 describes as the highest mode of knowing:

> <span class="verse-ref" data-verse="bg-18-55">*bhaktyā mām abhijānāti yāvān yaś cāsmi tattvataḥ / tato māṃ tattvato jñātvā viśate tad anantaram*</span>

"Through devotion one knows me truly, what I am and who I am. Having known me truly, one enters me thereupon." The operative verb is *abhijānāti*, from √jñā with the prefix *abhi*, indicating direct cognitive possession rather than inference or sensory perception. <Term iast="bhakti">Bhakti</Term> functions here as the medium of <Term iast="jñāna">jñāna</Term>.

*<Term iast="śāstra">Śāstra</Term>* (scripture): the corrective that prevents <Term iast="bhakti">bhakti</Term> from becoming projection. The <Term iast="chāndogya upaniṣad">Chāndogya's</Term> *ācāryavān puruṣo veda* (ChU 6.14.2), "a person with a teacher knows," establishes that Brahman-knowledge requires śāstric transmission. Scripture functions as <Term iast="pramāṇa">pramāṇa</Term>, preventing the <Term iast="bhakta">bhakta</Term> from constructing a personal <Term iast="brahman">Brahman</Term> shaped by his own psychology.

## The Epistemological Claim: Jñāna as Primary Pramāṇa

<Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya's</Term> recognition of Krishna as <Term iast="brahman">Brahman</Term> is, in the strictest technical sense, a **<Term iast="jñāna">jñāna</Term>-event** — a cognitive event structured by <Term iast="ātma vidyā">Ātma Vidyā</Term>. His perceptual apparatus had been transformed by <Term iast="brahma vidyā">Brahma Vidyā</Term>, the knowledge of <Term iast="brahman">Brahman</Term> that makes <Term iast="brahman">Brahman</Term> visible *as* <Term iast="brahman">Brahman</Term> rather than as a remarkable human being. The transformation of the cognitive instrument is prior to all of them.

The <Term iast="muṇḍaka upaniṣad">Muṇḍaka Upaniṣad</Term> states the mechanism with precision:

> <span class="verse-ref" data-verse="mundaka-3-2-3">*yam evaiṣa vṛṇute tena labhyas / tasyaiṣa ātmā vivṛṇute tanūṃ svām*</span>

"Whom this [<Term iast="ātman">ātman</Term>] chooses, by him alone is it obtained; to him this <Term iast="ātman">ātman</Term> reveals its own form." The <Term iast="ātman">ātman</Term> reveals itself only to one already oriented toward it. This is <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya's</Term> three conditions described in reverse: the self-disclosure of <Term iast="brahman">Brahman</Term> to a mind prepared by <Term iast="brahma vidyā">Brahma Vidyā</Term>. Recognition is that self-disclosure, triggered by the prepared instrument.

<Term iast="dhṛtarāṣṭra">Dhritarāṣṭra's</Term> failure of recognition follows directly. He is oriented toward <Term iast="sṛṣṭi">Sṛṣṭi</Term> — toward kingdom, succession, family, and power. His cognitive architecture is the opposite of <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya's</Term> first condition. The <Term iast="kaṭha upaniṣad">Kaṭha Upaniṣad</Term> is unambiguous about the consequence:

> <span class="verse-ref" data-verse="katha-1-2-23">*nāyam ātmā pravacanena labhyo / na medhayā na bahunā śrutena*</span>

"This <Term iast="ātman">ātman</Term> is obtained through neither instruction, nor intellect, nor extensive learning." The exclusion of *pravacana* (discourse), *medhā* (intellectual power), and *bahu-śruta* (scriptural accumulation) as sufficient conditions is radical. What remains is *vṛṇute*, the choosing — the <Term iast="ātman">ātman's</Term> self-disclosure to the one whose orientation is already aligned with it.

## The Sanatsujāta Section: Ātma Vidyā as Architectonic Theme

The <Term iast="udyoga parva">Udyoga Parva's</Term> placement of the <Term iast="sanatsujāta">Sanatsujāta</Term> section (UP 83-88) immediately preceding <Term iast="bhagavad yāna">Bhagavad Yāna</Term> is structural. <Term iast="vidura">Vidura</Term> brings <Term iast="sanatsujāta">Sanatsujāta</Term> specifically to instruct <Term iast="dhṛtarāṣṭra">Dhritarāṣṭra</Term> on <Term iast="brahma vidyā">Brahma Vidyā</Term> and <Term iast="ātman">ātman</Term>. The <Term iast="sanatsujāta parva">Sanatsujāta Parva</Term> is among the <Term iast="mahābhārata">Mahābhārata's</Term> most concentrated expositions of Upanishadic <Term iast="ātman">ātman</Term>-knowledge.

<span class="pullquote">Scripture is heard; Vidyā is absent. The manas receives information and fails to undergo transformation.</span>

<Term iast="dhṛtarāṣṭra">Dhritarāṣṭra</Term> receives the teaching and cannot act on it. The *<Term iast="manas">manas</Term>* receives information and fails to undergo transformation.

<Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya</Term>, by contrast, is <Term iast="vyāsa">Vyāsa's</Term> disciple. The *<Term iast="divya cakṣus">divya cakṣus</Term>* (divine sight) granted by <Term iast="vyāsa">Vyāsa</Term> is the Sanskrit tradition's idiom for the perceptual transformation that <Term iast="brahma vidyā">Brahma Vidyā</Term> produces — a cognitive reorganization rather than a magical ability grafted onto an unprepared mind. <Term iast="vyāsa">Vyāsa</Term> grants <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya</Term> *<Term iast="sarvajñatā">sarvajñatā</Term>*, complete cognition of the war's events. <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya's</Term> recognition of Krishna as <Term iast="brahman">Brahman</Term> precedes and underlies this grant. He reports the war truly because he already sees truly. The epistemology is prior to the narration — which is precisely why the <Term iast="gītā">Gītā</Term> places <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya</Term> as its outer witness rather than any other figure.

## What This Means Now

The <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya</Term>-<Term iast="dhṛtarāṣṭra">Dhritarāṣṭra</Term> exchange poses a direct challenge to contemporary Vedantic culture. The question "How do you recognize Brahman-in-form?" is the same question posed to every student who approaches a teacher claiming to embody <Term iast="brahman">Brahman</Term>. Contemporary answers almost never resemble <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya's</Term>.

They run: *I recognized him through his miracles. Through the peace in his presence. Through thousands of others who recognized him. Through prayers answered after surrender.* Every one of these is epistemologically identical to <Term iast="dhṛtarāṣṭra">Dhritarāṣṭra's</Term> position: orientation toward <Term iast="māyā">Māyā</Term>, toward the contents of <Term iast="sṛṣṭi">Sṛṣṭi</Term>, toward the psychological effects of devotion taken as primary evidence.

<Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya's</Term> answer requires <Term iast="ātma vidyā">Ātma Vidyā</Term> as a structural precondition of recognition. Two consequences follow.

<span class="pullquote">A mind oriented toward Māyā cannot recognize Brahman even when Brahman stands directly in front of it.</span>

First, this constitutes the sharpest available critique of the cult mechanism. The cult operates by substituting emotional attachment and social proof for the hard work of <Term iast="brahma vidyā">Brahma Vidyā</Term>. A mind oriented toward <Term iast="māyā">Māyā</Term> cannot recognize <Term iast="brahman">Brahman</Term> even when <Term iast="brahman">Brahman</Term> stands directly in front of it, as <Term iast="dhṛtarāṣṭra">Dhritarāṣṭra</Term> demonstrates perfectly. A mind shaped by <Term iast="ātma vidyā">Ātma Vidyā</Term> requires neither social consensus nor institutional authority nor miracle-verification.

Second, responsibility for recognition lies with the inquirer. The <Term iast="gītā">Gītā's</Term> <span class="verse-ref" data-verse="bg-7-19">*vāsudevaḥ sarvam iti sa mahātmā sudurlabhaḥ*</span> (BG 7.19), "such a <Term iast="mahātman">mahātman</Term> who sees Vāsudeva as all is exceedingly rare," speaks to the rarity of the prepared mind, not to the rarity of incarnations. <Term iast="brahman">Brahman</Term>, which is everywhere, is recognized by almost no one, because the instrument of recognition — <Term iast="ātma vidyā">Ātma Vidyā</Term> — is almost never developed.

## Coda: Sañjaya as the Gītā's Frame

The <Term iast="bhagavad gītā">Bhagavad Gītā</Term> begins with <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya</Term> narrating and ends with <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya</Term> summarizing. The framing is structural. <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya</Term> is the <Term iast="gītā">Gītā's</Term> epistemological witness — the one whose clarity of vision makes the <Term iast="gītā">Gītā's</Term> testimony trustworthy. His statement at BG 18.76-77 that he rejoices again and again recollecting this conversation (*hṛṣyāmi ca punaḥ punaḥ*) is a phenomenological report: the <Term iast="ānanda">ānanda</Term> that arises when <Term iast="jñāna">jñāna</Term> is intact and the seeing is clean.

The <Term iast="gītā">Gītā's</Term> last verse through <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya</Term> (18.78):

> <span class="verse-ref" data-verse="bg-18-78">*yatra yogeśvaraḥ kṛṣṇo yatra pārtho dhanurdharaḥ / tatra śrīr vijayo bhūtir dhruvā nītir matir mama*</span>

"Wherever Krishna the Lord of Yoga, wherever Pārtha the bowman: there, prosperity, victory, expansion, sound policy — this is my conviction." A mind organized by <Term iast="ātma vidyā">Ātma Vidyā</Term> sees clearly where the axis of reality runs and aligns with that axis rather than with the fluctuating surface of events. The verse describes a consequence, not a prediction.

<Term iast="dhṛtarāṣṭra">Dhritarāṣṭra</Term> never achieves this alignment. Every philosophical instrument for recognition is offered to him across the <Term iast="udyoga parva">Udyoga Parva</Term>: <Term iast="sanatsujāta">Sanatsujāta's</Term> <Term iast="brahma vidyā">Brahma Vidyā</Term>, <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya's</Term> living example, <Term iast="vidura">Vidura's</Term> practical wisdom, and finally Krishna himself in the assembly. He refuses each one. The refusal proceeds from the deepest form of <Term iast="māyā">Māyā</Term> — the kind that presents itself as duty, love, and loyalty while remaining orientation toward the impermanent dressed as the permanent.

The <Term iast="udyoga parva">Udyoga Parva's</Term> diagnostic question for every serious Vedantic inquirer: what is your actual orientation? <Term iast="sañjaya">Sañjaya's</Term> answer to <Term iast="dhṛtarāṣṭra">Dhritarāṣṭra</Term> is a mirror.
