## Sloka of the Day — BG 2.20

<VerseBlock
  ref="BG 2.20"
  devanagari="न जायते म्रियते वा कदाचित् नायं भूत्वा भविता वा न भूयः"
  iast="na jāyate mriyate vā kadācit nāyaṃ bhūtvā bhavitā vā na bhūyaḥ"
  translation="It is never born, nor does it die; having been, it never ceases to be."
>
  <p>
    The claim is not about the body. It is about the witness that is never an object of experience.
  </p>
</VerseBlock>

## One Term — atman

Atman names the witness, not a private psychological self. A common misuse is to treat it like a personality label.

## One Question

What remains when every object of experience is set aside?
