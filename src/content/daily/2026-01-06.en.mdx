## Sloka of the Day — BG 2.47

<VerseBlock
  ref="BG 2.47"
  devanagari="कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन"
  iast="karmaṇy-evādhikāras te mā phaleṣu kadācana"
  translation="You have the right to action alone, never to its fruits."
>
  <p>
    The verse is not a denial of outcome. It is a refusal to make outcome the motive.
  </p>
</VerseBlock>

## One Term — karma

Karma is action and its consequences. A common misuse is to reduce it to fate.

## One Question

Where do you feel the pull of outcome-driven action today?
